Who Was Jack Kilby?

Jack St. Clair Kilby, born November 8, 1923. Was an American, Electrical Engineer. He took part in the realization of the first integrated circuit in 1958.

What did Jack Kilby do?

Jack Kilby was part of the team to realize the first integrated circuit in 1958. Building on his success and striving towards the future, he was also co-inventor for the handheld calculator and thermal printer. He received the Nobel Prize in Physics on December 10th, 2000.

Why Jack Kilby will be remembered?

Ignoring the the patents Kilby had for: the plug-in-circuit units, the semiconductor structure fabrication, the miniature SIC and many more. Every item he designed is incorporated in every house, school, bussiness or any building on and off earth that has any connection with technology, be it as simple as a "handheld calculator". He paved the start of the technology era by starting to make technology more accessible and mobile. Be it the IC chip in a laptop, or the calculator your child uses at school. His time may have come through cancer on June 20th, but his legacy lives on as long as technology exists.
